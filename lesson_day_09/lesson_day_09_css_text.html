Briards

A Heart wrapped in fur

The briard or berger de brie, is a large breed of dog traditionally used as a herder and guardian of sheep.

Breed History

The biard, which is believed to have originated in France, has been bred for centuries to herd and to protect sheep. The breed was used by French Army as sentries, messengers and to search for wounded soldiers because of its fine sense of hearing. Biards were used in First World War almost to the point of extinction. Currently the population of biards is slowly recovering. Charlemagne, Napoleon, Thomas Jefferson, and Lafayette are all owned biards.
